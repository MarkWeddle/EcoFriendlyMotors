What to Know

What Affects EV Insurance Costs

Four factors explain most of the difference between EV and gas-car premiums — and between one insurer's quote and the next.

Insurance Costs

EV premiums are often 10–25% higher than an equivalent gas car, mainly because of higher purchase prices and repair costs. But the spread between insurers is even larger, so a driver who compares quotes frequently pays less for an EV than a non-shopper pays for a gas car.

Battery Cover

The battery pack is the most expensive component of an EV. Check that your policy covers battery damage from collisions and, ideally, charging-related incidents — and confirm how a leased or separately warranted battery is treated before you sign.

Repair Costs

EVs often need manufacturer-certified repair shops, specialized diagnostics and post-accident battery inspections, which raises claim costs. Insurers that run certified EV repair networks typically settle claims faster and price EV policies more competitively.

How to Compare Quotes

Get at least three quotes with identical coverage levels and deductibles. Ask specifically about battery cover, charging equipment cover, and any low-mileage or EV-specific discounts. Re-shop your policy every renewal — EV pricing is changing quickly as insurers gather more claims data.
